For seven years, Quidditch has been an important aspect in the lives of special a unique group of Pitt students. The bonds made over muddy practices, tournament weekends, and meals at Market have solidified many friendships since its creation. If you've ever been on a campus tour with one of the infamous Pitt Pathfinders, you probably already heard them mention the team. They just mention us for the little much-needed humor during the tour, but there's a lot more to "muggle" Quidditch than kids running around on brooms.


Believe it or not, universities and communities around the country have formed teams. Originating in Vermont, "muggle" Quidditch has spread across the country, eventually forming a United States Quidditch league. During a typical season, these teams verse one another in tournaments hosted by different universities. These tournaments lead up to regional championships. Some regions include the Mid-Atlantic, Southwest, and Great Lakes, which is where the Pitt team competes every year.
